Slide 2 · Methylation

The Folate → Methylation Pipeline

Folate must be converted through multiple steps before it can donate methyl groups. Two enzymes in your genome are variants — one creates a 30–40% throughput reduction at the most critical step.

Primary Variant: MTHFR C677T
Converts 5,10-methyleneTHF to 5-MTHF — the activated folate form that donates methyl groups. The C677T variant reduces enzyme activity by 30–40%. Every downstream process that needs methyl groups gets less.
Secondary Variant: MTHFS
MTHFS converts folinic acid (5-formyl-THF) back to THF for re-entry into the cycle. When this is impaired, folinic acid builds up unused and the cycle has less substrate. This is why folinic acid (not methylfolate) is the preferred supplement form.
Fix #1: Riboflavin (R5P)
MTHFR requires riboflavin (as FAD) as a cofactor. Masterjohn's key insight: adequate riboflavin largely rescues MTHFR C677T activity. This may be more important than supplementing extra methylfolate.
Fix #2: Hydroxocobalamin + Folinic Acid
MTRR recycles the B12 that MTR uses to remethylate homocysteine. MTRR impairment means B12 gets depleted faster. Hydroxocobalamin is the preferred form — metabolically neutral, body converts to whatever form is needed.
Slide 3 · Methylation

What SAM Powers (and What Starves)

SAM (S-adenosylmethionine) is the universal methyl donor. Every process below requires SAM to function. When the MTHFR pipeline runs at 60–70% capacity, all of these compete for the shortfall.

The Creatine Insight (Masterjohn)
Creatine synthesis via GAMT is the single largest consumer of SAM methyl groups — roughly 70% of all methylation in the body. If you supplement creatine directly, you bypass this demand entirely and free up SAM for everything else. This is the highest-leverage single supplement for MTHFR.
When SAM is Low: Priority Order
Your body doesn't evenly ration scarce SAM. Essential methylations (DNA, myelin) are prioritized. COMT activity drops — catecholamines clear more slowly, which amplifies anxiety/stress response. Phosphatidylcholine drops — membrane integrity suffers.
Slide 4 · Sulfur

The Transsulfuration Pathway

When homocysteine can't be remethylated back to methionine, it's shunted into the sulfur pathway. This produces glutathione, taurine, and H₂S — but your CTH variant reduces yield at a key step.

CTH Heterozygous Variant
CTH (cystathionine gamma-lyase) cleaves cystathionine into cysteine. A het variant reduces enzyme activity — not dramatically, but enough to matter when the pipeline is already stressed. Less cysteine means less GSH, less taurine, and more reliance on dietary sources of both.
H₂S: Signal vs. Toxin
H₂S is a legitimate gasotransmitter at low concentrations. At higher concentrations it inhibits Complex IV of the electron transport chain (same mechanism as cyanide, but milder). CoQ10 and molybdenum help the enzyme sulfite oxidase process H₂S → sulfite → sulfate for excretion.
Why Taurine Matters Here
Taurine is incorporated as a chemical modification in mitochondrial tRNAs — specifically for the tRNAs that translate the mtDNA-encoded subunits of Complex I and IV. Without adequate taurine, these subunits are mistranslated, directly impairing ETC function.
Supplement Targets
P5P (B6) supports CBS (entry step). Taurine directly compensates for reduced CTH output. Molybdenum is the cofactor for sulfite oxidase. CoQ10 supports H₂S clearance AND functions in Complex I/II/III electron transfer — dual role.
Slide 5 · Mitochondria

Mitochondrial Electron Transport Chain

NADH and FADH₂ feed electrons into the ETC. Electron flow powers proton pumping across the inner membrane, and that proton gradient drives ATP synthesis. Your ACAD9 variant impairs Complex I assembly.

ACAD9 — The Assembly Problem
ACAD9 is a bifunctional protein — it does fatty acid oxidation AND serves as an assembly factor for Complex I. The variant means fewer fully-functional Complex I complexes get assembled into the inner membrane. This is structural, not just enzymatic — you have fewer "machines" running. Riboflavin (FAD) is the cofactor and partially compensates.
Why Glucose vs. Fat Matters
Glucose oxidation produces NADH → feeds Complex I. Ketones (from fat) produce more NADH but bypass glycolysis — they also generate more FADH₂ (→ Complex II, bypasses Complex I). This is why ketogenic eating often improves energy in people with Complex I issues: it routes electrons around the broken assembly.
CoQ10 — The Electron Ferry
CoQ10 (ubiquinol) sits in the membrane and shuttles electrons from Complexes I and II to Complex III. It's also an antioxidant that neutralizes free radicals at the membrane. Low CoQ10 = traffic jam between I/II and III. The ubiquinol form (reduced) is the active electron carrier — this is why ubiquinol supplements are preferred.
MK-4 as Backup Electron Carrier
Vitamin K2 as MK-4 can function as an alternative electron carrier in the ETC — it can drain electrons back from Complex III under conditions of ETC stress. This was documented in Masterjohn's corpus as a mitochondrial function separate from its clotting/bone roles. Low-dose MK-4 (not MK-7) is the relevant form.
Slide 6 · Vitamin D

Vitamin D: Three Sequential Hits

Active vitamin D requires three steps: liver hydroxylation, transport through blood, and receptor binding in target tissues. You have genetic variants affecting all three steps — each independently reduces effective D at the cell level.

Hit 1: CYP2R1 (Liver)
CYP2R1 performs the first hydroxylation step — converting D3 to 25-OH-D₃ (the storage form measured in blood tests). A variant here means you produce less 25-OH-D₃ from the same sun exposure or dietary intake. Your blood D level will run lower than someone with the same input.
Hit 2: GC Gc2 Haplotype (Transport)
The GC globulin protein carries 25-OH-D₃ through the blood. The Gc2 haplotype (rs4588 G/G + rs7041 C/C) has the weakest binding affinity of all three haplotypes. D is more loosely held, clears faster, and delivers less to target tissues. This is independent of your serum D level.
Protocol Implication
Because you lose D at two sequential steps before kidney activation, standard D dosing may be insufficient. Aim for higher 25-OH-D blood levels (60–80 ng/mL vs. standard 40–60) and include vitamin K2 MK-4 to direct calcium appropriately. Test, don't guess.
Note on Kidney Step
CYP27B1 (kidney) does the final activation to 1,25-OH-D₃. This step appears normal in your data. The bottlenecks are upstream. The active form then binds VDR in target cell nuclei to regulate gene expression — hundreds of genes are downstream of this axis.
Slide 7 · Protocol

Where Each Supplement Intervenes

Every supplement in the protocol targets a specific broken step in one of these pathways. This is the mechanistic map — not theory, not general wellness.

Start Sequence
Week 1: Hydroxocobalamin + Folinic Acid (Seeking Health combo lozenge). Week 2: Add Riboflavin R5P. Week 3: Add Creatine + TMG. Week 4: Add CoQ10, Taurine, Molybdenum, P5P. Ongoing: Titrate methylfolate only after baseline is stable.
Highest Leverage Moves
1. Creatine → frees 70% of SAM demand. 2. Riboflavin → restores MTHFR + ACAD9. 3. Hydroxocobalamin → fixes MTRR B12 cycling. 4. Taurine → compensates CTH and directly supports ETC. These four address the root causes, not just symptoms.
