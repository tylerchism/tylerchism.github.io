Slide 1 · Methylation

The Folate Reduction Pathway

Dietary folate must be converted through four enzymatic steps before it can donate methyl groups. The MTHFR step is your most significant bottleneck — impaired by a C677T variant and dependent on riboflavin as a cofactor.

What Breaks
C677T variant reduces MTHFR efficiency by 30–40%. The enzyme converts 5,10-methylene-THF → 5-MTHF but only functions with riboflavin (FAD) bound. Without adequate B2, the enzyme is inactive even if present.
Interventions
(1) Riboflavin R5P restores MTHFR function. (2) Folinic acid (5-formyl-THF) bypasses MTHFR and enters the pool at the 5,10-methenyl-THF step, but the MTHFS variant (rs6495446) also reduces this conversion. Both variants compound the downstream 5-MTHF shortage.
Slide 2 · Methylation

The Methionine / SAM Cycle

SAM (S-adenosylmethionine) is the universal methyl donor. This cycle regenerates methionine from homocysteine, synthesizes SAM, and distributes methyl groups to hundreds of reactions. MTHFR impairment upstream starves this entire cycle.

When Cycle Slows
Reduced 5-MTHF (from MTHFR impairment) means MTR cannot efficiently remethylate homocysteine. Hcy accumulates. SAM production drops. Downstream methylation reactions are all affected — including creatine synthesis (~70% of all SAM consumption), DNA methylation, and neurotransmitter metabolism.
Interventions
(1) Riboflavin fixes MTHFR → more 5-MTHF → more remethylation. (2) TMG (betaine) drives the BHMT bypass directly. (3) Creatine supplementation removes the largest single SAM consumer, immediately relieving pressure on the cycle. These three together are the highest-leverage moves.
Slide 3 · Sulfur Metabolism

Transsulfuration: Homocysteine to Cysteine and Beyond

Homocysteine exits the methionine cycle into the transsulfuration pathway via two B6-dependent enzymes. The CTH variant reduces cysteine output, limiting three critical downstream branches: glutathione, taurine, and H₂S.

CTH Variant Effect
The CTH heterozygous variant reduces cystathionase activity. Less cysteine is produced from cystathionine, which in turn limits all three downstream branches: glutathione (GSH), taurine synthesis, and the CBS/CTH-derived H₂S pool. Supplementing P5P (active B6) supports both CBS and CTH.
Why GSH and Taurine Matter
Glutathione is the primary intracellular antioxidant; reduced levels worsen oxidative stress and impair detoxification. Taurine is incorporated into mitochondrial tRNA as a chemical modification required for proper translation of Complex I and IV subunits — reduced taurine directly impairs ETC protein synthesis.
Slide 4 · Sulfur Clearance

H₂S Oxidation: Mitochondrial Clearance Chain

Hydrogen sulfide must be oxidized through a three-enzyme chain to reach its safe excreted form (sulfate). Each enzyme has a specific inorganic cofactor requirement. When CoQ10 or molybdenum is limiting, toxic intermediates accumulate.

When This Chain Backs Up
H₂S in excess inhibits Complex IV of the ETC (cytochrome c oxidase), blocking electron transport. Sulfite, if SUOX is limiting, is a neurological irritant. This creates a feedback loop: impaired ETC → less CoQ10 cycling → slower H₂S clearance → more Complex IV inhibition.
Supplement Targets
CoQ10 (as ubiquinol, 100–200 mg/day): directly restores SQR electron acceptor capacity. Molybdenum (100–300 mcg/day): cofactor for SUOX, enables sulfite → sulfate conversion. These are the two direct interventions in this chain.
Slide 5 · Mitochondria

Mitochondrial Electron Transport Chain

NADH and FADH₂ feed electrons into the ETC. Electron flow powers proton pumping across the inner membrane, and that proton gradient drives ATP synthesis. Your ACAD9 variant impairs Complex I assembly — fewer functional CI complexes are built.

ACAD9 Defect
ACAD9 is required to assemble Complex I from its 45 subunits. Without adequate ACAD9 activity, fewer functional CI complexes are built. NADH cannot enter the chain efficiently. This reduces proton pumping across CI, lowers the H⁺ gradient, and decreases ATP synthase output. Riboflavin (as FAD) is the cofactor that makes ACAD9 functional.
Why This Matters for Energy
Complex I handles ~40% of all NADH entering the ETC. A 20–30% reduction in CI capacity meaningfully reduces total ATP production. This explains the chronic low energy and poor exercise recovery pattern — it’s a mitochondrial throughput problem, not just substrate availability.
Slide 6 · Vitamin D

Vitamin D Activation: Three Sequential Impairments

Active vitamin D (calcitriol) requires sequential enzymatic steps from skin to liver to kidney, plus transport through blood. You carry variants that impair the liver hydroxylation step and reduce the binding affinity of the transport protein — a compounding deficit.

Three Sequential Hits
Each step is impaired: (1) CYP2R1 variant reduces liver 25-hydroxylation — less calcidiol made. (2) Gc2 haplotype reduces binding protein affinity by ~50% — less calcidiol reaches the kidney. (3) Combined effect means far less calcitriol (active form) is produced per IU of D₃ consumed compared to someone without these variants.
Why D Is Critical
Active calcitriol (1,25-OH₂D₃) regulates over 200 genes. Relevant to this profile: immune system regulation (including innate immunity, relevant to inflammation), calcium homeostasis, and gene expression in mitochondria-dense tissues. The standard “normal” range (30 ng/mL) is insufficient for this genotype — aim for 60–80 ng/mL.
